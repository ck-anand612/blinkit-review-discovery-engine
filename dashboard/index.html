Top Discovery Insights

Q1 · Why Users Stick to Familiar Categories
Users default to familiar categories because they value convenience, fast delivery, and having everything in one place — not because they lack interest in new ones.
"good but doesn't have varieties of Mother Dairy products"
15 reviews · Habit Loop
Q2 · Main Barriers to Category Exploration
High prices vs. local stores, frequent stockouts, and poor search functionality are the primary blockers preventing users from exploring new categories.
"The product variety is also limited in my area, so I can't find the brands I'm looking for."
12 reviews · Friction
Q3 · How Discovery Happens
Product discovery happens through organic browsing of the app interface rather than targeted search — users stumble upon categories, not seek them actively.
"please have some more variety"
15 reviews · Discovery Mode
Q5 · What Users Need Before Trying New Categories
Before trying a new category, users need competitive pricing, reassurance about product quality, and a verified wide range of options in their area.
"you should make beauty products available"
7 reviews · Trust Signal
Q7 · Most Willing Explorers
Users who already prioritize convenience, fast delivery, and variety are most willing to experiment with new categories — primed for cross-category growth.
"wide range and nice price"
8 reviews · Growth Segment
Q8 · Most Significant Unmet Needs
Clearest unmet needs: a broader product catalog, improved search optimized for open-ended discovery, and more competitive pricing on non-grocery items.
"it is good but, it doesn't have varieties..."
10 reviews · Product Gap

Strongest Signal — Community Evidence

🛡️
Trust & Authenticity Barriers to Category Exploration
Extracted from 134 curated Reddit & forum discussions · Secondary evidence corroborating Play Store discovery clusters

Pain Points

  • Receiving fake, damaged, or low-quality products
  • Difficulty with returns and refund processes
  • Overall lack of trust for non-grocery purchases
  • Used/unsealed items delivered as new
  • "Free sample, Not for Sale" items sold at full price

PM Opportunities

  • Implement robust quality control & authenticity verification
  • Streamline returns & refunds to lower exploration risk
  • Add verified seller badges and product review layers
  • Surface detailed product information before purchase
  • Consider authenticity guarantees for beauty/electronics

Key Hypotheses for Interview Validation (Part 2)

Hypothesis 1 · Trust & Authenticity
Users hesitate to buy high-value or personal care items because they doubt product authenticity and find returns harder than on Amazon/Flipkart.
🔴 Highest Impact
Hypothesis 2 · Assortment & Search
Users want to explore new categories but abandon the effort because of limited brand variety and a search UX optimized for known grocery items — not open discovery.
🟣 High Impact
Hypothesis 3 · Price Perception
Users view Blinkit as premium-priced for non-grocery items and need clear competitive pricing or discounts to break the habit of relying on local stores.
🟡 Medium Impact
Hypothesis 4 · Quality Assurance
Lack of detailed product information and user reviews creates a high entry barrier for unfamiliar categories where evaluating quality is essential before buying.
🟢 Medium Impact
